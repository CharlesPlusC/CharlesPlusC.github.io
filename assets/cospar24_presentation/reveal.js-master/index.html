An Evaluation of Physics-Based Force Model Performance in LEO

Implications for Next-Generation Space Traffic Management

Goal

  • Question: If I were an SSA/STM orbit provider, how would I approach the force modelling aspect of my orbit generation? What would my recipe be?
  • Method: Sensitivity analysis of force model fidelity to SSA Key Performance Indicators.

Metrics

  • Orbit Determination RMS
  • Orbit Propagation RMS
  • Probability of Collision

  • 20-minute arcs (1 state vector/minute)
  • Batch Least Squares Orbit Determination with each force model
  • Orbit Propagation with each force model (6 hours)
  • ~ 1450 OD/OP runs

Obvious Takeaways

  • Epoch Space environment conditions should drive force model selection.
    • Lower altitudes and higher solar/geomagnetic activity degrade OD/OP significantly.
    • For high solar/geomagnetic activity, drag modelling is crucial but still involves significant variability.

Not So Obvious Takeaways

  • Many OD/OP performance enhancements likely remain underexploited:
    • Gravity field resolution enhances OD fit at least as much as drag models. Under all conditions.
  • Solid and ocean tides provide the single best improvement in cross-track propagation RMS (orbital tubes).
  • As we exit solar maximum, conservative and radiation forces will become an increasingly reliable way to improve orbit propagation.
    • Drag models become increasingly useless and in some instances even detrimental (Sentinel-2/3), particularly at higher altitudes.

Operational Perspective

  • Why should I care? What are the practical/operational implications of this?
    • Let's take a look at the impact on probability of collision...

Collision Simulation

  • Go to t+12hrs (in the SP3-C ephemeris) after the first state calculated in the OD run
  • Take the state, flip the velocity vector and generate keplerian orbit
    • Now I have a trajectory that I know collides with my truth trajectory at t+12hrs!
  • Go back to t0
  • Sample the covariance generated by each force modelling strategy 10,000 times
  • Propagate each state vector with its corresponding force model for 12.1 hours
    • Calculate the time and distance of closest approach for each trajectory relative to the truth trajectory
    • Note: Traveling at ~7.5km/s, a 1/1000th second error in TCA is equivalent to 7.5m. In many cases this is ~= the size of the spacecraft.

Sentinel-2A: Low Solar Activity

  • Solid and Ocean Tides provides the best improvement in DCA.
    • Suggests most of the error was in the cross-track.
  • Interesting to note some linearity disappears with addition of DTM
    • Consistent with propagation experiments where DTM performed best at high altitude.

Sentinel-2A: High Solar Activity

  • DCA is ~2x that of low solar activity case. TCA is now unbiased.
  • Distribution less Gaussian
  • Addition of Drag model not greatly helpful
    • But computationally much more expensive...

GRACE-FO-A: Low Solar Acitivity

  • Largely unbiased TCA. DCA is better than high altitude counterparts
  • Improved Gravity Field Greatly Improves DCA estimate
    • Hypothesis: the strength of gravity forcing at this altitude offsets the errors in drag
  • Dominance of drag increases uncertainty (wider spread and non-linear distribution)
    • All drag models perform adequately

GRACE-FO-A: High Solar Acitivity

  • DCA 2x as bad. TCA 10x as bad.
  • Much less normally distributed
  • Gravity field improvement virtually useless
  • Drag is most important factor at play.
  • Of the drag models JB08 outperforms other by ~2x in DCA and TCA, but has a wider spread

Conclusions

    • The space environment changes distinctly with altitude and solar acitivity
    • The behaviour of certain KPIs will exhibit different behaviour under different circumstances
      • Likely that below a certain drag signal threshold, gravitational and radiative force modelling are underexploited in SSA/STM
    • Drag modelling is very important at present, and will almost always be below ~500km.
    • Entering the next solar minima (2030+), imrpovements in conservative and radiative forces are likely to become more significant.
